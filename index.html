ISO Technical Committees and ISO/TC 301

The ISO operates through Technical Committees (TC) responsible for specific scopes. The scope of ISO/TC 301 is energy management and energy savings with ISO 50001 as its focal point. In the United States the member organization to the ISO is the American National Standards Institute (ANSI).

When a TC has a scope of interest to organizations in the U.S., ANSI accredits a Technical Advisory Group (TAG) representing the U.S. to that TC. These TAGs develop the U.S. position on their respective TC activities and ballot the interim and final approval of relevant documents within a TC. The U.S. TAG to ISO/TC 301 serves as the national forum for U.S. input to ISO/TC 301.

About the U.S. TAG to ISO/TC 301

The U.S. TAG to ISO/TC 301 operates in accordance with the requirements of ANSI and the ISO as the U.S. TAG responsible for representing U.S. positions on energy management, efficiency, and savings standards being developed by the International Standards Organization (ISO). It is composed of energy management experts willing to devote time and energy to the TC process to develop standards useful to U.S. industry, commercial enterprises and other organizations.

TAG Activities

The U.S. TAG, following ANSI and ISO procedures, provides input on international standards drafts and ballots on approving documents to become international standards. Input is provided at the various stages in standards development through consensus which is the basis of standardization.

In addition to developing standards, the U.S. TAG provides input on clarification, initiation, continuation or withdrawal of ISO/TC 301 standards. The U.S. TAG also provides input on related documents from other ISO Technical Committees who are working in the areas related to energy, energy efficiency, energy savings, emissions management, etc.
