What is the U.S. Technical Advisory Group to ISO TC 301?

Strategic Energy Management (SEM) is practiced by many organizations to improve energy performance, save costs, and reduce unwanted emissions. While SEM can take many forms, the international standard for SEM is provided by ISO 50001:2018 â€“ Energy Management developed under the procedures of the International Standards Organization (ISO). It is this form of SEM that has international recognition as representing superior management practice.

The ISO operates through Technical Committees (TC) responsible for specific scopes. ISO 50001 is in the scope of TC 301, the technical committee responsible for standards on energy management and determining energy savings. In the United States the member organization to the ISO is the American National Standards Institute (ANSI). ANSI accredits Technical Advisory Groups (TAG) for the TCs with scopes of interest to organizations in the U.S. These TAGs develop the position of the U.S. on their respective TC activities and ballot the interim and final approval of relevant documents within a Technical Committee. In the United States, the national inputs to ISO TC 301-Energy Management and Energy Savings is the U.S. TAG to 301 (TAG 301).

The U.S. Technical Advisory Group

The US Technical Advisory Group (TAG) to ISO Technical Committee (TC) 301 is the official TAG accredited by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) responsible for representing US positions on energy management, efficiency, and savings standards being developed by the International Standards Organization (ISO). It is composed of energy management experts willing to devote time and energy to the TC process to develop standards useful to U.S. industry, commercial enterprises and other organizations.

Activities

The US TAG, following ANSI and ISO procedures, provides input on international standards drafts and votes on approving documents to become international standards. Input is provided at the various stages in a standards development either through on-line processes managed by the ISO or ANSI or in-person meetings with other countries delegates. The intent is to determine consensus which is the basis of the final standard.

In addition to developing standards, the USTAG provides input on interpretation, clarification, continuation and withdrawal of ISO TC 301 standards.

Standards Development

Provide input on international standards drafts at various stages through online processes managed by ISO or ANSI, or in-person meetings with delegates from other countries.

Consensus Building

Work to determine consensus, which is the basis of final international standards, ensuring U.S. perspectives are represented in the global process.

Voting & Approval

Vote on approving documents to become international standards, including interim and final approval of relevant TC 301 documents.

Interpretation & Clarification

Provide input on interpretation and clarification of ISO TC 301 standards to ensure proper understanding and implementation.

Standards Review

Participate in decisions regarding continuation, revision, and withdrawal of existing ISO TC 301 standards.

U.S. Representation

Represent U.S. positions on energy management, efficiency, and savings standards being developed by ISO.

ISO TC 301 Standards

The standards on which the USTAG has worked can be found at the ISO TC 301 catalogue. These include ISO 50001, standards giving additional guidance and clarification on the components of ISO 50001, and standards providing guidance for auditing and certifying conformance with ISO 50001.
